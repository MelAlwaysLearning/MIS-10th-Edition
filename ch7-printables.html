Chapter 7 Bingo — Call List & Instructions

ISM3011 | Read the CLUE aloud — students identify the term on their card

How to Play

  1. Distribute one Bingo card (pages 4–6) per student. Cards 1–4 have different layouts.
  2. Read the clue aloud. Do NOT say the term in parentheses.
  3. Students mark the matching term on their card.
  4. First to get 5-in-a-row calls "BINGO!" Verify and award a prize.

Prize suggestions: extra credit, snack, skip-one-homework pass, front-of-room seating.

Call List
E-Commerce — "Buying and selling goods or services over the internet — the transaction itself"
E-Business — "Broader use of internet technology to run ALL business processes — includes but goes beyond sales"
B2C — "Business-to-Consumer: a company selling directly to individual customers — like Amazon or Netflix"
B2B — "Business-to-Business: companies selling to other companies — 5× larger in volume than B2C"
C2C — "Consumer-to-Consumer: individuals selling to each other through a platform — like eBay or Poshmark"
G2C — "Government-to-Consumer: government agencies delivering services online — like the IRS portal or DMV"
EDI — "Electronic Data Interchange: automated computer-to-computer B2B transactions in standardized format"
Digital Wallet — "Software that securely stores payment info so you don't re-enter it — Apple Pay, PayPal, Google Pay"
Revenue Model — "The strategy a company uses to earn money from its online product or service"
Subscription Model — "Recurring fee for continued access — Netflix, Spotify Premium, Microsoft 365"
Freemium — "Free basic version plus a paid premium tier that removes limits or adds features — Duolingo, Zoom"
Affiliate Model — "Earning a commission by referring customers to another company's product — Amazon Associates"
Shopping Cart — "The digital mechanism that holds selected items before checkout — abandonment rate is ~70%"
SSL/HTTPS — "Encryption protocol for web transactions — the padlock in your browser — required for any site handling payments"
Supply Chain — "The end-to-end process of producing and delivering a product from supplier to customer"
M-Commerce — "Mobile commerce — buying and selling via smartphones; 73% of e-commerce traffic in 2023"
Social Commerce — "Buying directly within social media platforms — Instagram Shopping, TikTok Shop"
Marketplace — "An e-commerce platform where multiple sellers list products — Amazon Marketplace, Etsy, eBay"
Storefront — "A company's own e-commerce website where they sell directly — Nike.com, Apple.com"
Conversion Rate — "The percentage of website visitors who complete a purchase — industry average is 2–4%"
B2G — "Business-to-Government: companies selling to government agencies — defense contractors, IT vendors"
Disintermediation — "Removing the middleman — manufacturers selling direct to consumers, bypassing retailers"
Pay-Per-Use — "Pay only for what you consume — AWS compute hours, Uber rides, iCloud storage"
Advertising Model — "Free service to users; revenue comes from selling ad space to businesses — Google, YouTube, Facebook"
FREE SPACE — "Everyone marks the center square!"
