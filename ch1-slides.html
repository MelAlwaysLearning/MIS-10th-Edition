📚 Core Concept

The IS Hierarchy — Who Uses What?

Click each level for a real company example ↓

Top Executives (CEO, CFO)
EIS — Executive Information System
🏢 Walmart's CEO views one dashboard showing global store performance, regional inventory, and market trends — all in real time.
Senior / Middle Managers
DSS — Decision Support System
✈️ American Airlines uses a DSS to analyze weather, crew schedules, and fuel costs to decide whether to reroute or delay flights.
Department Managers
MIS — Management Information System
☕ Starbucks' district manager reviews weekly reports: which stores hit targets, employee hours logged, and complaint trends by location.
Front-Line Operations (every day, every second)
TPS — Transaction Processing System
💳 Every tap of your card at McDonald's: a TPS processes payment, updates inventory, and records the sale — millions of times per day.

📚 Know the Difference

IT vs IS — Don't Mix These Up

⚙️ IT (Information Technology)

  • Hardware + Software + Networks
  • The tools and infrastructure
  • Example: the iPhone itself
  • Example: AWS cloud servers
  • Example: a 5G tower

🔄 IS (Information System)

  • IT + People + Data + Processes
  • The whole system in action
  • Example: Uber's entire platform
  • Example: FIU's student portal
  • Example: a hospital's patient records
💡 Quick Rule: IT is the engine. IS is the whole car — including the driver, the road, and the destination.
