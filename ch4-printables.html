Chapter 4 Bingo — Call List & Instructions

ISM3011 | Read the CLUE aloud — students identify the term on their card

How to Play

  1. Distribute one Bingo card (pages 4–7) per student. Cards 1–4 have different layouts.
  2. Read the clue aloud. Do NOT read the term in parentheses.
  3. Students mark the matching term on their card.
  4. First to get 5-in-a-row calls "BINGO!" Verify and award a prize.

Prize suggestions: extra credit point, snack, skip-one-quiz pass, preferred exam seat.

Call List
CIA Triad — "The three-pillar security framework: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability"
Confidentiality — "Only authorized people can see the data — preventing unauthorized access or disclosure"
Integrity — "Data is accurate and hasn't been tampered with — it can be trusted"
Availability — "Authorized users can access data and systems when they need to"
Phishing — "Fake emails or websites designed to steal your credentials"
Spear Phishing — "A targeted phishing attack using your name, company, or personal details"
Ransomware — "Malware that encrypts your files and demands payment to restore access"
Trojan — "Malicious code disguised as legitimate software"
Spyware — "Software that silently monitors your activity and sends data to attackers"
DDoS — "Flooding a server with traffic until it crashes — targeting Availability"
Social Engineering — "Psychological manipulation to trick humans into handing over access"
Firewall — "The network gatekeeper that filters incoming and outgoing traffic"
Encryption — "Scrambling data so only authorized parties with the key can read it"
2FA — "Requiring two verification types to log in — blocks 99.9% of automated attacks"
VPN — "Creates an encrypted tunnel for your internet traffic on public Wi-Fi"
Antivirus — "Detects, quarantines, and removes known malware from your system"
Biometric Auth — "Using your fingerprint, face, or iris to verify identity — something you ARE"
BCP — "Business Continuity Plan — how an org keeps running during and after a disruption"
Disaster Recovery — "The IT-specific plan to restore systems and data after an incident"
Data Breach — "Unauthorized access to or disclosure of protected information"
Zero-Day — "A vulnerability unknown to the software vendor — no patch exists yet"
Patch Management — "Keeping software updated to fix known security vulnerabilities"
Intrusion Detection — "Systems that monitor network traffic for signs of an attack in progress"
Backup — "The best defense against ransomware — restore data without paying the ransom"
FREE SPACE — "Everyone marks the center square!"
