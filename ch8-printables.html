Chapter 8 Bingo — Call List & Instructions

ISM3011 | Read the CLUE aloud — students must identify the term on their card

How to Play

  1. Distribute one Bingo card (pages 4–6) to each student. Cards 1–4 have different layouts.
  2. Read the clue aloud. Do NOT read the term in parentheses.
  3. Students mark the matching term on their card using a pen, marker, or coin.
  4. First student to get 5-in-a-row calls "BINGO!" Verify and award a prize.

★ Starred terms are quiz answers — they appear on EVERY card.

Call List
RFID ★ — "Radio Frequency Identification — uses radio waves to automatically track tagged objects with no line-of-sight"
M-commerce ★ — "E-commerce conducted via smartphones or tablets — not a desktop"
NFC ★ — "Near Field Communication — the technology behind tap-to-pay; range is only about 4 centimeters"
Bluetooth ★ — "Short-range wireless that connects your headphones, keyboard, and car audio to your phone"
Mobile Hotspot ★ — "Using your phone's cellular data to create a personal Wi-Fi network for other devices"
Wi-Fi — "Wireless LAN technology that connects devices to the internet within a building — your home router uses this"
3G/4G/5G — "Generations of cellular networks — 5G delivers 100× faster speeds than 4G with under 1ms latency"
Bandwidth — "The maximum data transfer rate of a connection — measured in Mbps or Gbps"
GPS — "Global Positioning System — satellite-based location tracking used in every map and navigation app"
Wearables — "Smart devices worn on the body — Apple Watch, Fitbit, and fitness trackers"
BYOD — "Bring Your Own Device — employees using personal phones and laptops for work"
Mobile Security — "Protecting smartphones from malware, phishing, and unauthorized access"
QR Code — "A 2D barcode scanned by a phone camera to access a link, menu, or payment"
Cellular Network — "Infrastructure of towers that provides phone calls and mobile data coverage over wide areas"
IoT — "Internet of Things — everyday devices like thermostats, smart speakers, and cars connected to the internet"
Contactless Payment — "Paying by tapping a card or phone — uses NFC technology underneath"
Mobile App — "Software designed specifically to run on smartphones or tablets"
Digital Wallet — "App that stores payment cards and IDs — Apple Pay and Google Pay are examples"
Radio Waves — "Electromagnetic waves that carry data through the air — the foundation of ALL wireless technology"
WAP — "Wireless Application Protocol — the early standard for accessing internet on mobile phones before smartphones"
Mobile Banking — "Performing bank transactions — transfers, deposits, payments — through a smartphone app"
Tethering — "Sharing your phone's internet connection with another device via USB or Bluetooth cable"
Wireless Network — "A network that uses radio waves instead of cables to connect devices"
Smartphone — "A mobile phone with internet access, apps, and computing capabilities — the device that enables m-commerce"
FREE SPACE — "Everyone marks the center square!"
