Chapter 2 Bingo — Call List & Instructions

ISM3011 | Read the CLUE aloud, not the term — students must recall the term

How to Play

  1. Distribute one Bingo card (pages 4–7) to each student. Cards 1–4 have different layouts.
  2. Read the clue aloud (bold text below). Do NOT read the term in parentheses.
  3. Students mark the matching term on their card.
  4. First student to get 5-in-a-row (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) calls "BINGO!"
  5. Verify by reading back the terms for each marked square. Award a prize.
  6. Continue for 2nd and 3rd winners, or clear boards and play again.

Prize suggestions: extra credit point, candy, skip-one-homework pass, front-of-line seating.

Call List
Competitive Advantage — "Producing better products or at lower cost than rivals so customers choose you"
Business Process — "A network of activities that produces a product or service for a customer"
Porter's Five Forces — "The framework that analyzes industry competitiveness from five angles"
Competitive Rivalry — "The intensity of competition among existing firms in an industry"
Threat of New Entrants — "How easy it is for new competitors to enter a market"
Threat of Substitutes — "The likelihood customers will switch to a different type of product"
Buyer Power — "The influence customers have to push prices down"
Supplier Power — "The influence vendors have to raise their prices on a firm"
Value Chain — "The linked set of activities through which a firm creates value"
Primary Activities — "The five core steps that directly create a product: inbound logistics through service"
Support Activities — "HR, IT, procurement, and infrastructure that enable the core steps"
Inbound Logistics — "Receiving and storing raw materials before production"
Outbound Logistics — "Delivering finished products to customers"
Operations — "Transforming inputs into the final product or service"
BPR — "Radical redesign of a business process to achieve dramatic improvements"
Incremental Improvement — "Small, continuous tweaks to an existing process"
Strategic IS — "An information system that gives a long-term competitive advantage"
Cost Leadership — "Competing by being the lowest-cost producer in the industry"
Differentiation — "Competing by offering a unique product or service rivals can't copy"
Focus / Niche — "Targeting a specific segment of the market with tailored offerings"
Margin — "The profit left over after all value chain costs are subtracted"
Switching Costs — "The expense or hassle a customer faces when changing providers"
Industry Analysis — "Examining the forces that determine profitability in a market"
RFID — "The Walmart technology used to track every item in inventory automatically"
