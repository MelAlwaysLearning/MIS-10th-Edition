Chapter 3 Bingo — Call List & Instructions

ISM3011 | Read the CLUE aloud — students must identify the term on their card

How to Play

  1. Distribute one Bingo card (pages 4–7) to each student. Cards 1–4 have different layouts.
  2. Read the clue aloud. Do NOT read the term in parentheses.
  3. Students mark the matching term on their card using a pen, marker, or coin.
  4. First student to get 5-in-a-row calls "BINGO!" Verify and award a prize.

Prize suggestions: extra credit point, candy, attendance waiver, front-row seating choice.

Call List
IT Ethics — "Standards of right and wrong for collecting, using, and sharing information"
PII — "Data that can uniquely identify a specific individual — like your SSN or fingerprint"
GDPR — "Europe's privacy regulation that requires informed consent and allows deletion requests"
HIPAA — "The US law protecting your personal health information at hospitals and clinics"
FERPA — "The law that prevents your university from sharing your grades without permission"
COPPA — "Requires parental consent before collecting data from children under 13"
CCPA — "California's state-level privacy law — the closest US equivalent to GDPR"
Right to be Forgotten — "The ability to demand a company permanently erase all data it holds about you"
Informed Consent — "Users must clearly agree to data collection — buried fine print doesn't count"
Privacy — "Your right to control who can see and use your personal information"
Intellectual Property — "Creative works like software, music, and code that are legally protected from copying"
Software Piracy — "Using software without a valid license — costs the industry billions each year"
Copyright — "Legal protection that gives the creator exclusive rights over their digital work"
Data Accuracy — "The ethical obligation to keep data correct and up-to-date — wrong data harms people"
Digital Divide — "The gap between those who have access to technology and those who don't"
Biometrics — "Physical identifiers like fingerprints or face scans — permanent PII that can never be changed"
Data Breach — "When unauthorized parties gain access to protected personal data"
Opt-In — "Data collection is OFF by default — users must actively choose to allow it (GDPR model)"
Opt-Out — "Data collection is ON by default — users must actively choose to stop it (common US model)"
Algorithmic Bias — "When an automated system produces discriminatory outcomes, even without intentional programming"
Phishing — "Tricking users via fake emails or sites into revealing passwords or sensitive data"
Encryption — "Scrambling data so only authorized parties can read it — a key privacy protection tool"
Plagiarism — "Presenting someone else's digital work — code, text, or ideas — as your own"
Ethics vs. Legal — "Something can be legal but still unethical — and vice versa"
FREE SPACE — "Everyone marks the center square!"
