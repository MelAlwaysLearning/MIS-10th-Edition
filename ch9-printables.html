Chapter 9 Bingo — Call List & Instructions

ISM3011 | Read the CLUE aloud — students must identify the term on their card

How to Play

  1. Distribute one Bingo card (pages 4–6) to each student. Cards 1–4 have different layouts.
  2. Read the clue aloud. Do NOT read the term in parentheses.
  3. Students mark the matching term on their card using a pen, marker, or coin.
  4. First student to get 5-in-a-row calls "BINGO!" Verify and award a prize.

★ Starred terms are quiz answers — they appear on EVERY card.

Call List
Web 2.0 ★ — "The interactive era defined by user-generated content and two-way participation — the 'read-write' web"
Crowdsourcing ★ — "Obtaining work, ideas, or solutions from a large online community — not an outsourced company"
Wiki ★ — "A collaborative website that anyone can edit — Wikipedia is the most famous example"
Social Networking ★ — "Platforms where users create profiles, connect, and share content within a network of personal connections"
Social Computing ★ — "Using social media and online communities to benefit businesses — like crowdsourcing product ideas from customers"
Blog — "A regularly updated website with entries in chronological order — personal or business journal format"
Podcast — "Audio content distributed digitally via subscription using an RSS feed"
User-Generated Content — "Content created by unpaid users — reviews, photos, videos, Wikipedia articles"
Viral Marketing — "Content that spreads rapidly through user sharing — the Ice Bucket Challenge raised $220M this way"
Social Commerce — "Buying products directly within social media platforms — TikTok Shop, Instagram Shopping"
RSS Feed — "A standardized format that automatically delivers new content to subscribers — used for podcasts and news"
Folksonomy — "A user-generated classification system created through tagging — hashtags are the most visible example"
Mashup — "A web application that combines data from multiple sources — Google Maps + real estate listings"
Social Media — "Platforms that enable users to create and share content and participate in social networking"
Hashtag — "A keyword tag that organizes content on social media and creates searchable communities"
Influencer — "A person with a large social media following who can drive purchasing decisions through recommendations"
Online Community — "A group of people interacting around shared interests on the internet — the foundation of crowdsourcing"
Microblog — "A short-format blog — Twitter/X is the most famous example with its character-limited posts"
Digital Identity — "Your online presence and reputation — the sum of your profiles, posts, and activity across platforms"
Tagging — "Adding keywords to digital content to organize and make it searchable — the building block of folksonomy"
Social CRM — "Using social media platforms to manage and improve customer relationships"
Filter Bubble — "An algorithmic echo chamber — you only see content that confirms what you already believe"
Open Source — "Software with publicly available source code that anyone can view, modify, and distribute"
Web 1.0 — "The original 'read-only' web — companies published static content and users could only consume it"
FREE SPACE — "Everyone marks the center square!"
