Chapter 6 Bingo — Call List & Instructions

ISM3011 | Read the CLUE aloud — students identify the term on their card

How to Play

  1. Distribute one Bingo card (pages 4–6) per student. Cards 1–4 have different layouts.
  2. Read the clue aloud. Do NOT say the term in parentheses.
  3. Students mark the matching term on their card.
  4. First to get 5-in-a-row calls "BINGO!" Verify and award a prize.

Prize suggestions: extra credit, snack, skip-one-homework pass, front-of-room seating.

Call List
LAN — "A network that covers a single building or campus — like this classroom's Wi-Fi"
WAN — "The largest type of network — spans countries or the entire globe; the internet is one"
MAN — "A network that covers a city or metropolitan area"
PAN — "A network covering just a few meters around one person — like Bluetooth earbuds"
TCP/IP — "The suite of protocols that is the universal language of the internet"
OSI Model — "A 7-layer framework showing how data travels from one device to another"
Bandwidth — "The capacity of a network connection — measured in bits per second"
Router — "Directs data packets between different networks using IP addresses"
Switch — "Connects devices within the same LAN — smarter than a hub, sends only to the right device"
Modem — "MOdulator-DEModulator: translates digital signals for travel over cable or phone lines"
Wi-Fi — "Wireless LAN technology using radio waves; standard 802.11; range up to 300 feet"
Ethernet — "A wired LAN standard using twisted-pair copper cable — the cables in offices and labs"
Packet — "A small chunk of data that travels independently across a network and is reassembled at the destination"
Protocol — "A set of rules that govern how devices communicate on a network"
Intranet — "A private network using internet technology accessible only to an organization's employees"
Extranet — "An extended intranet that allows selected external partners to access certain resources"
VPN — "Creates an encrypted tunnel through the public internet for secure remote access"
Firewall — "Monitors and filters network traffic to block unauthorized access and attacks"
ISP — "Internet Service Provider — the company that gives you internet access (Comcast, AT&T)"
5G — "Fifth-generation cellular network — up to 10 Gbps; enables IoT, autonomous vehicles, smart cities"
Bluetooth — "Short-range wireless PAN standard — ~30 feet; used for earbuds, keyboards, speakers"
NFC — "Near Field Communication — 4 cm range; used for Apple Pay and contactless tap payments"
IP Address — "A unique numerical address assigned to every device on a network — like a street address for data"
Network Topology — "The physical or logical layout/arrangement of devices and connections in a network"
FREE SPACE — "Everyone marks the center square!"
